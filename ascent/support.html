Ascent: Support

Need Help?

We are here to help you get the most out of Ascent. Check the common questions below, or reach out to us directly. To see what the app covers and how the climb works, visit the Ascent page.

Unlocking the Full Course

Ascent is free to download and free to play through Camp One (foundations, the language of algebra, and all of linear equations). To climb past it, you unlock the Full Expedition: a single one-time purchase, made inside the app through the App Store. There is no subscription, and one unlock covers every climber profile on the device. You buy it in the app, not on this website.

1

Open the unlock screen

There are two ways in. On the mountain screen, tap the The Full Expedition card that appears once you reach the top of Camp One. Or open Settings and choose For grown-ups. Both lead to the same place.

2

Pass the grown-ups check

A quick "For a grown-up" arithmetic question keeps the purchase in adult hands. Answer it to continue. If it is answered wrong, the app simply asks a grown-up to help, and nothing is charged.

3

Tap Unlock and pay

The Unlock button shows the one-time price for your country. Tap it, and Apple's own purchase sheet appears. Confirm with Face ID, Touch ID, or your Apple Account password. Payment is handled entirely by Apple using the payment method on your Apple Account; we never see your card details.

4

Keep climbing

The rest of the mountain unlocks right away, with no restart. If a parent needs to approve first through Ask to Buy, the unlock activates automatically once they do.

Already bought it? On a new device, or after reinstalling, open the same screen and tap Bought it already? Restore purchases. while signed in with the Apple Account that made the purchase. The unlock comes back at no extra charge.

Redeeming an Unlock Code

If you have an unlock code, such as a launch promo we shared, you can redeem it in the app to unlock the Full Expedition. A promotional code may make the unlock free.

1

Open the unlock screen

Reach it the same way as a purchase: the The Full Expedition card on the mountain screen, or Settings → For grown-ups. Pass the quick grown-ups check to continue.

2

Tap "Have a code? Redeem it here."

You will find this link below the Unlock and restore options on the unlock screen.

3

Enter your code

Apple's redemption sheet opens. Type or paste your code, and the Full Expedition unlocks as soon as it is accepted.

Codes are redeemed through your Apple Account, so make sure you are signed in to the account you want the unlock tied to. Each code can be used once.

Common Questions

Who is Ascent for?

Anyone taking or retaking Algebra 1, roughly ages 13 and up. Fifty-six skills across twelve legs cover a full US Algebra 1 course, from foundations to quadratics and radicals. Reaching the summit means finishing the course. For ages 8 to 11 we make Numina, our arithmetic game.

I already know some algebra. Do I have to start at the bottom?

No. When a climber starts their expedition, Ascent offers the flight in: a short, optional placement check that can start you as high as Camp Two, with everything below credited in full. If you are new to algebra, skip it and begin the classic route at Lukla. The check adapts as you answer and ends kindly at the first miss, so it never becomes a test you can fail.

What does Ascent cost?

Ascent is free through Camp One: foundations, the language of algebra, and all of linear equations. A single one-time purchase, the Full Expedition, unlocks the rest of the mountain. There is no subscription, no ads, and nothing else to buy.

How do I restore my purchase on a new device?

The Full Expedition is tied to your Apple ID. On a new device, open the unlock screen and tap Restore Purchases, and make sure you are signed in with the same Apple ID that bought it. The unlock also works for every climber profile on the device.

Does the purchase work with Family Sharing and Ask to Buy?

Yes. The Full Expedition supports Family Sharing, so one purchase can cover your family group. Ask to Buy is supported too: a teen can request the unlock, and it activates automatically once a parent approves.

I'm stuck on a problem. What do I do?

Call Base Camp. The radio button is on every problem and is never punished: it gives four levels of help, from a small nudge to the full worked idea. Every concept also has a lesson you can reread and fresh worked examples, and the scratchpad gives you space for rough work with your finger or Apple Pencil.

Why am I being asked to review old skills?

That is the acclimatization system doing its job. Ascent schedules spaced reviews of earlier skills and mixes them into sessions, because coming back to something right before you would forget it is what makes it stick. Altitude in Ascent means a skill is truly owned, not just seen once.

What is the Weather Window?

A short daily challenge, separate from the main climb, with its own streak. It is the same for every climber on a given day, so you can compare cards. Missing a day never takes away altitude or patches.

Can more than one person use Ascent?

Yes. Ascent supports multiple climbers on one device, each with their own expedition: separate progress, altitude, streaks, and patches. Switch climbers from the profile button on the mountain screen. Profiles are not accounts and need no sign-up, and one Full Expedition unlock covers all of them.

Does Ascent work offline?

Yes. The whole app works offline. An internet connection is only used for optional iCloud sync between your own devices and for the App Store purchase itself.

Does it sync between iPhone and iPad?

Yes. If you enable iCloud for Ascent, progress syncs across your own iPhone and iPad through your private iCloud account, which we cannot access. You can turn this off in your device settings if you prefer to keep each device separate.

Where is my summit certificate?

After you summit, the certificate is yours to revisit any time from Settings, and you can share it with the standard iOS share sheet. Your expedition patches live in the patch gallery, also in Settings.

The app crashed or is not responding

Make sure you are running the latest version of Ascent and iOS or iPadOS. Try force-closing the app and reopening it, and if needed restart the device. If the problem continues, contact us with a short description of what you were doing when it happened.
